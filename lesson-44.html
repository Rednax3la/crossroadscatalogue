Understanding Emotions & Regulation

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: November 2, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens understand their emotions from a biblical perspective, identify healthy ways to process feelings, and develop practical strategies for emotional regulation.
1. Emotional Check-in (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Weather Report"

Teens describe their current emotional state using weather metaphors:

"My emotions feel like..."
(Sunny, stormy, cloudy, etc.)
"The temperature of my feelings is..."
(Hot, cold, warm, freezing)
"My emotional forecast for today is..."

Discussion: "Just like weather, emotions are natural and constantly changing. Today we'll learn how to navigate our emotional climate."

2. Biblical View of Emotions (15 min)
15 min
A. Emotions in the Bible

Biblical examples of emotional expression:

  • Jesus wept (John 11:35)
  • David's joy and lament in Psalms
  • Paul's contentment in all circumstances
  • Elijah's depression and renewal

Closing Prayer: "Heavenly Father, thank you for creating us as emotional beings. Help us to honor you with our feelings, bringing them to you in prayer. Teach us healthy ways to process and express our emotions. Fill us with your peace that passes understanding. Amen."

Emotion Regulation Tips
  • Name your emotions to tame them
  • Create a "calm down" toolkit (music, journal, etc.)
  • Practice the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique
  • Talk to trusted adults about big feelings
  • Remember emotions are temporary states, not permanent identities
