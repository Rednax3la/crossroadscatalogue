Daily Date with God

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: March 23, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens establish a consistent daily devotional life by understanding the importance of personal time with God, creating practical routines, and overcoming common obstacles to consistency.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Perfect Date Brainstorm"

Teens describe their ideal date with God in small groups:

Where would you meet?
What time of day?
What would you do together?
What would you talk about?

Discussion: "Just like we plan special time with friends, we need to intentionally plan daily time with God. Today we'll learn how to make this a consistent habit."

2. The Why and How (15 min)
15 min
A. Why Daily Time Matters

The importance of consistent devotion:

  • Spiritual nourishment and growth (Matthew 4:4)
  • Developing intimacy with God
  • Guidance for daily decisions
  • Strength during challenges
"But seek first his kingdom and his righteousness, and all these things will be given to you as well." Matthew 6:33
B. Elements of a Meaningful Devotion

What to include in your daily date:

Prayer
Scripture
Reflection
Journaling
Worship
"Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path." Psalm 119:105
C. Making It Personal

Customizing your devotional time:

  • Find your best time of day
  • Create a dedicated space
  • Use tools that work for you
  • Start with manageable time commitments
"Come near to God and he will come near to you." James 4:8

Planning Exercise: Teens create their personalized devotional plan for the coming week, selecting times and activities.

4. Overcoming Obstacles (10 min)
10 min
Roadblocks & Solutions
"I don't have enough time"
Start with 5 minutes. Quality over quantity.
"I get distracted easily"
Find a quiet space. Put devices away.
"I don't know what to do"
Use a devotional guide or Bible app.
"I keep forgetting"
Set a daily alarm. Make it a routine.

30-Day Devotional Challenge

Commit to meeting with God daily for the next month. Track your progress!

Day 1-7: Establish
Day 8-14: Deepen
Day 15-30: Own It
"Let the morning bring me word of your unfailing love, for I have put my trust in you. Show me the way I should go, for to you I entrust my life." Psalm 143:8

Closing Prayer: "Heavenly Father, thank you for inviting us into daily relationship with you. Give us the discipline to prioritize time with you, the wisdom to hear your voice, and the joy of experiencing your presence each day. May our daily dates with you become the anchor of our lives. Amen."

Facilitator Tips
  • Share your personal devotional routine and challenges
  • Recommend devotionals for teens: "Jesus Calling for Teens", "Our Daily Bread Teens Edition"
  • Suggest Bible apps: YouVersion, Bible Gateway, Glorify
  • Create a "Quiet Time Corner" in your meeting space
  • Follow up next week to hear about their experiences
