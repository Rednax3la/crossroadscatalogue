God's Design for Dating

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: October 12, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To teach teens biblical principles for healthy relationships and dating, emphasizing God's design, the importance of boundaries, and how to honor God in relationships.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Relationship Spectrum"
Friendship

Casual hanging out

Dating

Intentional relationship with purpose

Courtship

Relationship with marriage in view

Discussion: "Where do you see yourself on this spectrum? What are the differences between these relationship types?"

2. Biblical Foundations (15 min)
15 min
A. God's Purpose for Relationships

Understanding relationships from God's perspective:

  • Relationships should honor God (1 Corinthians 10:31)
  • Designed for mutual encouragement and growth
  • Preparation for marriage (if that's God's plan)
  • Reflecting Christ's relationship with the Church
"So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God." 1 Corinthians 10:31
B. Qualities to Look For

Characteristics of a godly relationship partner:

  • Growing relationship with Christ
  • Respectful toward parents and authority
  • Shared values and beliefs
  • Positive influence on your faith
  • Emotional maturity
"Do not be yoked together with unbelievers. For what do righteousness and wickedness have in common? Or what fellowship can light have with darkness?" 2 Corinthians 6:14
C. Setting Healthy Boundaries

Establishing relationship guardrails:

Communication
Time Limits
Group Settings
Physical Boundaries
"Flee the evil desires of youth and pursue righteousness, faith, love and peace, along with those who call on the Lord out of a pure heart." 2 Timothy 2:22
3. Practical Application (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Scenario Responses"
Scenario 1: "Your boyfriend/girlfriend wants to spend every afternoon together, but you have responsibilities at home and church. How do you respond?"
Scenario 2: "You feel pressured to move faster physically than you're comfortable with. What boundaries could you set?"
Scenario 3: "Your friends are dating, but you don't feel ready. How do you handle the social pressure?"
Scenario 4: "You notice your boyfriend/girlfriend is becoming possessive. What steps should you take?"
Relationship Readiness Quiz
1. Am I dating to gain popularity or because I genuinely care about this person?
2. Would my parents and spiritual leaders approve of this relationship?
3. Is this relationship drawing me closer to God?
4. Commitment & Closing (10 min)
10 min
Relationship Covenant

My Relationship Commitment

I commit to honoring God in my relationships by:

Seeking God First
Setting Boundaries
Involving Parents
Honoring Others
"Above all else, guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it." Proverbs 4:23

Closing Prayer: "Lord, thank you for creating relationships. Give us wisdom to navigate dating in a way that honors you. Help us set boundaries that protect our hearts and keep you at the center of our lives. Guide us in building relationships that reflect your love and bring glory to your name. Amen."

Dating Wisdom for Teens
  • Date with purpose, not just for fun
  • Keep group dates until you're more mature
  • Involve your parents in the relationship
  • Set physical boundaries and stick to them
  • If you wouldn't marry them, why date them?
