iLead Leadership Development

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: June 8, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens discover and develop their leadership potential through biblical principles, self-awareness activities, and practical leadership challenges.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Leadership Spotting"

Teens identify leadership moments in their lives:

"A time I took initiative was..."
"Someone I admire as a leader because..."
"A challenge where I helped others was..."
"I show leadership when I..."

Discussion: "Leadership isn't just about position - it's about influence, service, and making a difference. Today we'll explore how you can develop your leadership potential."

2. Biblical Leadership (15 min)
15 min
A. Servant Leadership Model

Jesus redefined leadership:

  • Leading by serving others (Mark 10:42-45)
  • Humility over hierarchy
  • Empowering rather than controlling
  • Leading with love and compassion
"Whoever wants to become great among you must be your servant, and whoever wants to be first must be slave of all." Mark 10:43-44
B. Types of Leaders

Different leadership styles:

Servant Leader
Visionary Leader
Team Builder
Organizer
"There are different kinds of gifts, but the same Spirit distributes them." 1 Corinthians 12:4
C. Leadership Qualities

Essential characteristics of godly leaders:

  • Integrity and honesty
  • Courage to do what's right
  • Responsibility and reliability
  • Good communication skills
  • Empathy and compassion
"But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, forbearance, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control." Galatians 5:22-23
3. Leadership Development (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "My Leadership Profile"
Your Name
Emerging Leader

Discover your leadership strengths:

Communication: How well do you express ideas?
Empathy: How well do you understand others' feelings?
Responsibility: How reliable are you?
Problem Solving: How do you handle challenges?
Leadership Scenarios
Scenario 1: Your group can't agree on a service project. What do you do?
Leadership Approach: Facilitate a discussion where everyone shares ideas, look for common themes, and help the group find a compromise that incorporates multiple perspectives.
Scenario 2: A friend is being left out of activities. How do you respond?
Leadership Approach: Reach out to the person directly, include them in your activities, and gently encourage others to be more inclusive.
Scenario 3: You notice a team member isn't contributing. What's your approach?
Leadership Approach: Privately ask if everything is okay, offer support, and help identify ways they can contribute using their strengths.
4. Leadership Challenge (10 min)
10 min
iLead Challenge

30-Day Leadership Challenge

Commit to practicing one leadership action each day:

Day 1-5: Practice active listening
Day 6-10: Take initiative to help
Day 11-15: Encourage someone
Day 16-20: Solve a problem
Day 21-25: Lead a small activity
Day 26-30: Mentor someone
"Don't let anyone look down on you because you are young, but set an example for the believers in speech, in conduct, in love, in faith and in purity." 1 Timothy 4:12

Closing Prayer: "God, thank you for creating each of us with leadership potential. Help us to develop the qualities of servant leaders like Jesus. Give us courage to step up, wisdom to lead well, and compassion to serve others. May we make a positive difference in our world. Amen."

Leadership Development Tips
  • Find a mentor who exemplifies good leadership
  • Read biographies of great leaders
  • Take on small leadership roles at home, school, or church
  • Reflect daily on leadership opportunities
  • Practice public speaking in safe environments
