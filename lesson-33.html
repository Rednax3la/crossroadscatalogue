Drugs Substance Abuse

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: August 17, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To educate teens about the dangers of drug and substance abuse, provide strategies for resisting peer pressure, and emphasize the importance of maintaining a healthy body as God's temple.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Myth vs. Fact"

Teens sort statements about drugs into myth or fact:

"Marijuana is harmless because it's natural"
"You can't get addicted after just one use"
"Drugs help you cope with stress"
"Prescription drugs are safer than illegal drugs"

Discussion: "Many myths about drugs can lead to dangerous choices. Today we'll separate fact from fiction and learn how to make wise decisions."

2. Understanding Substance Abuse (15 min)
15 min
A. Common Substances & Effects

Overview of frequently abused substances:

Marijuana
Prescription Drugs
Nicotine/Vaping
Alcohol
"Do you not know that your bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit?" 1 Corinthians 6:19
B. Consequences of Abuse

How substance abuse impacts life:

Physical Effects

Brain damage, heart problems, addiction, overdose

Mental Effects

Anxiety, depression, psychosis, poor decision-making

Social Effects

Damaged relationships, isolation, legal troubles

Spiritual Effects

Distance from God, loss of purpose, moral compromise

"Can a man scoop fire into his lap without his clothes being burned?" Proverbs 6:27
3. Resistance Strategies (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Peer Pressure Scenarios"

Teens practice responding to peer pressure situations:

"Everyone's trying it - just take one puff!"
"This pill will help you study better for exams"
"Don't be a baby - one drink won't hurt"
"It's just vaping - it's not even a real drug"
Simple "No"
Walk Away
Change the Subject
Strength in Numbers

Refusal Skills Practice

Role-play different responses to peer pressure:

  • Be direct and assertive
  • Suggest alternatives
  • Use humor to deflect
  • Blame parents or faith commitments
4. Healthy Alternatives (10 min)
10 min
Commitment to Health

Healthy Living Challenge

Replace risky behaviors with positive alternatives:

Exercise & Sports
Creative Arts
Community Service
Faith Activities
"Do not conform to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind." Romans 12:2

Closing Prayer: "Heavenly Father, thank you for creating our bodies as your temple. Give us wisdom to recognize the dangers of substance abuse and the courage to resist peer pressure. Help us find healthy ways to cope with stress and fill our lives with activities that honor you. Protect our friends and community from the devastation of addiction. Amen."

Resources for Help
  • National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357)
  • Talk to a trusted adult, pastor, or counselor
  • Support groups for teens
  • Online resources at samhsa.gov
