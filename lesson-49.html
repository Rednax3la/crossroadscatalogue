Service

Duration: ~60 minutes
Date: November 15, 2026
Age Group: 13–14 years
Objective: To understand service not as a religious duty or a resume item, but as the natural overflow of a life shaped by Jesus — exploring why we serve, what it costs, and what it produces in us and others.
1. Opener: The Towel (10 min)
10 min
Read the Room

Read John 13:3–5 aloud — the scene where Jesus wraps a towel around his waist and washes his disciples’ feet.

“Jesus knew that the Father had put all things under his power… so he got up from the meal, took off his outer clothing, and wrapped a towel around his waist… and began to wash his disciples’ feet.”John 13:3–5

Ask: “Why is this weird? What would you expect someone who has ‘all things under his power’ to do?”

Land on this: Jesus’ power was what made his service possible — not what made it beneath him.

2. Why We Serve (15 min)
15 min

Two Kinds of Service

Performance
Serving to be seen, to earn approval, to feel good about yourself. It burns out fast and breeds resentment.
Overflow
Serving because you’ve been served first — because gratitude needs somewhere to go. This is sustainable.
We serve because we were served first

The logic of the gospel runs in one direction: God gives, we receive, we give. Service isn’t how we earn God’s approval — it’s how gratitude finds expression in the world.

“We love because he first loved us.”1 John 4:19
We serve because people are worth seeing

Much of service is simply noticing. Noticing who is on the edge of the group. Noticing who is carrying something heavy. Noticing who nobody else is talking to. That act of noticing is already service.

3. What Service Actually Looks Like (15 min)
15 min
It Fits Who You Are

Service isn’t one-size-fits-all. It takes different shapes depending on how you’re wired.

Practical service: fixing, building, organising, showing up early and setting up
Relational service: being present, checking in, remembering details, listening well
Creative service: using music, art, words, or design to create something for others
Behind-the-scenes service: the work that makes everything else possible and goes unnoticed

Ask: “Which of these feels most like you? Which feels least natural? Why?”

4. What Service Does to You (10 min)
10 min
Service shapes character

Every act of service fights the pull toward self-centredness. Not because service is painful, but because it trains your attention outward — away from your own comfort, your own schedule, your own reputation — and toward someone else.

Over time, that rewires how you see the world. Servants notice things that self-focused people miss entirely.

Service builds community

A group where everyone is looking out for themselves is exhausting. A group where people serve each other becomes something people want to belong to. Your service contributes to the kind of community Crossroads is trying to be.

5. Challenge & Close (10 min)
10 min
Specific, Not Vague

The problem with “I’ll serve more” as a commitment is that it’s easy to forget. Today each person identifies one specific act of service to do in the next seven days — a named person, a named action.

This Week’s Challenge

Write down: Who will you serve? What will you do? When will you do it? Share it with one person in this room. Next week, report back.

“Whoever wants to become great among you must be your servant.”Mark 10:43

Closing Prayer: “Lord, thank you that you didn’t sit at a distance — you came down. Help us follow you out of the comfortable seat and into the lives of the people around us. Show us who to serve this week, and give us the courage to actually do it. Amen.”
