Why ROPEs?

January 18, 2026 50 minutes Ages 13–14 Lesson 2

Just as life events mark growth, ROPEs create intentional markers for our spiritual development — helping teens see how God uses rites of passage to strengthen faith.

10 min

Section 1: Opening Icebreaker

Activity: “Life’s Milestones”
“What was a significant first in your life? (First day of school, first time riding a bike, etc.)”
“How did you feel before, during, and after that experience?”
“Was there anyone who helped guide you through that transition?”

Just as these life events mark growth, ROPEs create intentional markers for our spiritual development. “Today we will explore how God uses rites of passage to strengthen our faith.”

15 min

Section 2: Understanding ROPEs

A. What are Rites of Passage?

Intentional experiences marking transitions. Three phases:

  • Separation: Leaving the familiar
  • Transition: Learning and growth
  • Incorporation: Returning with new status
“When I was a child, I talked like a child, I thought like a child, I reasoned like a child. When I became a man, I put the ways of childhood behind me.”
1 Corinthians 13:11
B. Biblical ROPEs Examples
  • Abraham’s covenant (Genesis 17)
  • Israel’s Passover (Exodus 12)
  • Jesus’ baptism (Matthew 3:13–17)
  • Bar Mitzvah at age 13
“As soon as Jesus was baptized, he went up out of the water. At that moment heaven was opened, and he saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove and alighting on him.”
Matthew 3:16–17
C. Why Modern Teens Need ROPEs
  • Clear spiritual milestones
  • Community recognition
  • Personal identity formation
  • Faith ownership
“This day I call the heavens and the earth as witnesses against you that I have set before you life and death, blessings and curses. Now choose life.”
Deuteronomy 30:19
20 min

Section 3: ROPE Creation Activity

Activity: “Three-Stranded ROPE”
Strand 1: Faith Heritage

Choose a colour representing your spiritual foundation.

  • Who introduced you to faith?
  • What spiritual inheritance have you received?
Strand 2: Current Journey

Choose a colour representing your present walk with God.

  • Where are you now spiritually?
  • What challenges are you facing?
Strand 3: Future Faith

Choose a colour representing your spiritual future.

  • What kind of believer do you want to become?
  • What spiritual milestones do you hope to reach?
Facilitator Note

Provide three colours of yarn for each teen. Explain how three strands represent Ecclesiastes 4:12 — “A cord of three strands is not quickly broken.”

10 min

Section 4: Commitment & Closing

Activity: ROPE Dedication

Invite teens to tie knots representing:

  • One knot for a childhood faith practice to release
  • One knot for a mature faith practice to embrace
  • One knot for a spiritual mentor to seek
Weekly Challenge

Share your three-stranded rope with a parent or mentor this week and explain what each strand represents in your spiritual journey.

Scripture
“Therefore, since we are surrounded by such a great cloud of witnesses, let us throw off everything that hinders and the sin that so easily entangles. And let us run with perseverance the race marked out for us.”
Hebrews 12:1
Closing Prayer

“Lord, as we braid these strands of faith, help us see our spiritual journey as a sacred passage. Give us courage to release childhood ways and embrace the maturity you have for us. May these ROPEs strengthen us for the journey ahead. Amen.”

Facilitator Tips
  • Share a personal ROPE experience from your own teen years
  • Bring rope examples to show the group
  • Play “Oceans” by Hillsong softly during the braiding activity
  • Provide beads or charms to mark each knot
  • Set up a ROPES Wall to display completed ropes
