Elements of the Conversation

Duration: ~60 minutes
Date: March 15, 2026
Age Group: 13–14 years
Objective: To explore what makes a conversation genuinely good — starting from everyday human experience — and apply those same principles to prayer, so teens move from performance to real dialogue with God.
1. Opener: What Makes a Great Chat? (10 min)
10 min
The Unread Message

Ask the group: “Think of the best conversation you’ve had recently — in person, a voice note, anything. What made it so good?”

Take 3–4 responses. Common answers: they actually listened, I could be real, it didn’t feel forced, we picked up where we left off.

Bridge: “Every one of those things is possible with God. Today we look at the five elements that make it happen.”

2. The Five Elements (30 min)
30 min
1
Presence
Showing up fully — not just physically

Think about texting someone while half-watching a video — they can tell. Conversations go flat when one person isn’t really there.

You
yeah sorry lol what were you saying

Prayer works the same way. Before saying anything, the first move is arriving — deliberately putting aside whatever else is on your mind and choosing to be here.

Try this:

Start your next prayer with 30 seconds of silence. Just acknowledge: “God, I’m here.” No agenda. Just presence.

2
Honesty
No performance, no formal speech

We often talk to God like we’re in a job interview — careful, polished, slightly stiff. But that’s not how you talk to someone you actually trust.

You (honest)
I don’t even know if I believe this is working. I’m saying it anyway.

The Psalms are full of this kind of honesty — complaints, frustration, confusion, joy. God isn’t looking for the right words. He’s looking for the real you.

“Trust in him at all times, you people; pour out your hearts to him, for God is our refuge.”Psalm 62:8
3
Listening
The underrated half of every conversation

Imagine a friend who talks for ten minutes and hangs up before you get a word in. That’s how most prayer goes — a monologue dressed up as a conversation.

Listening in prayer looks like: staying quiet after you’ve spoken, reading Scripture and asking “what are you saying to me here?”, noticing what keeps surfacing in your thoughts in the silence.

Try this:

After finishing your prayer, stay quiet for two minutes. Write down any thought, word, or image that comes to mind. Over time, you’ll notice patterns.

4
Continuity
Good conversations build — they don’t reset

When you talk to a close friend, you don’t re-introduce yourself every time. You continue: “Remember what I was stressed about last week? Here’s what happened.”

You
So that thing I was worried about — it worked out. Thank you.

A prayer journal builds this. When you write down what you prayed for and what happened, prayer stops being isolated requests and starts becoming a living story you can look back on.

5
Returning
Showing up even when it feels one-sided

Every close relationship has stretches where one person carries more of the conversation. You keep showing up anyway — not because it always feels good, but because the relationship matters.

Dry seasons in prayer are completely normal. The thing that carries you through them isn’t a new technique — it’s simply returning. Tomorrow. And the day after that.

“Let us not become weary in doing good, for at the proper time we will reap a harvest if we do not give up.”Galatians 6:9
3. Group Reflection (10 min)
10 min
Which Element Do You Need Most?
Which of the five feels most natural to you in prayer already?
Which one do you find hardest — and why do you think that is?
Is there someone in your life who models one of these elements really well?
4. Personal Challenge (10 min)
10 min
Pick One, Practise It

Each teen picks the one element they want to deliberately work on this week. Write it down and share with one other person in the room for accountability.

This Week’s Challenge

Choose your one element. Every day this week, spend 5 minutes in prayer and intentionally apply it. By Sunday, notice: did anything feel different?

“Come near to God and he will come near to you.”James 4:8

Closing Prayer: “Father, teach us to talk with you the way we talk with someone we fully trust. Help us show up, be honest, and learn to listen. And on the days it feels dry — give us the grace to return. Amen.”
