Confirmation & Baptism

Duration: 60 minutes
Date: April 6, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens understand the significance of baptism and confirmation in the Christian faith, explore their personal readiness for these sacraments, and appreciate the commitment involved in publicly declaring their faith.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min

Discussion: "Today we'll explore how these symbols help us understand baptism and confirmation - two important steps in our faith journey."

2. Understanding the Sacraments (20 min)
20 min

Baptism: New Life in Christ

  • Symbolizes cleansing from sin
  • Represents dying to old self and rising to new life
  • Public declaration of faith
  • Entry into the Christian community
"Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit." Matthew 28:19

Confirmation: Sealed with the Spirit

  • Personal affirmation of baptismal vows
  • Receiving the gifts of the Holy Spirit
  • Commitment to live as an adult in the faith
  • Strengthening for Christian service
"When the apostles in Jerusalem heard that Samaria had accepted the word of God, they sent Peter and John to Samaria. When they arrived, they prayed for the new believers there that they might receive the Holy Spirit." Acts 8:14-15
The Meaning Behind the Rituals

Baptism: Not just about water, but about spiritual rebirth and joining Christ's body

Confirmation: Not just a ceremony, but about personal ownership of faith and receiving spiritual empowerment

"He saved us, not because of righteous things we had done, but because of his mercy. He saved us through the washing of rebirth and renewal by the Holy Spirit." Titus 3:5

Facilitator Tips
  • Share your own baptism/confirmation story
  • Invite recently confirmed teens to share their experiences
  • Provide information about upcoming baptism/confirmation dates
  • Have pastors available for one-on-one conversations
  • Use water, oil, and other tangible symbols during the lesson
