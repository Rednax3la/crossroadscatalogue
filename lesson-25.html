Problem Solving God's Way

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: June 22, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To equip teens with biblical problem-solving skills and strategies, teaching them to approach challenges with wisdom, faith, and practical steps grounded in God's Word.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Problem Puzzles"

Teens share solutions to these common teen challenges:

"You forgot an important assignment is due tomorrow..."
"A friend is mad at you for something you didn't do..."
"You're overwhelmed with responsibilities..."
"You broke something valuable at home..."

Discussion: "We all face problems - but how we solve them makes all the difference. Today we'll learn God's approach to problem solving."

2. Biblical Problem Solving (15 min)
15 min
A. God's Perspective on Problems

How God uses challenges in our lives:

  • Problems develop our character (Romans 5:3-4)
  • Challenges teach us dependence on God
  • Difficulties prepare us to help others
  • God promises wisdom to those who ask
"Consider it pure joy, my brothers and sisters, whenever you face trials of many kinds, because you know that the testing of your faith produces perseverance." James 1:2-3
B. The STEP Method

A biblical approach to problem solving:

1
Seek God First

Pray for wisdom and guidance (James 1:5)

2
Think Through

Gather information and understand the problem

3
Explore Options

Brainstorm possible solutions with godly counsel

4
Put into Practice

Choose the best solution and take action

"Trust in the LORD with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight." Proverbs 3:5-6
3. Problem Solving Practice (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Real-Life Scenarios"
Scenario 1: Your best friend has started hanging out with a group that's involved in some risky behaviors. You're worried but don't want to lose your friendship.
Scenario 2: You're struggling in math class. Your parents expect A's, but you're barely passing. You feel overwhelmed and stressed.
Scenario 3: You accidentally shared something private about a friend that's now spreading through school. Your friend is hurt and angry.
Scenario 4: You've been invited to a party where you know there will be alcohol. You don't want to go, but you're worried about being left out.

Select a scenario above to develop a solution using the STEP method

4. Problem Solver's Commitment (10 min)
10 min
Prayer and Commitment

7-Day Problem Solving Challenge

Day 1: Identify a current problem
Day 2: Pray specifically for wisdom
Day 3: Seek biblical guidance
Day 4: Talk to a wise person
Day 5: Brainstorm solutions
Day 6: Choose and implement a solution
Day 7: Reflect on what you learned
"If any of you lacks wisdom, you should ask God, who gives generously to all without finding fault, and it will be given to you." James 1:5

Closing Prayer: "Lord, thank you for promising us wisdom when we face problems. Help us to remember to seek you first in every challenge. Give us discernment to see problems as opportunities to grow in faith and character. Guide us as we apply your principles to solve problems in our daily lives. Amen."

Problem Solving Tips
  • Write down the problem to clarify your thinking
  • Break big problems into smaller, manageable parts
  • Consider the consequences of each solution
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help
  • Learn from each problem-solving experience
