Happy Life Visit

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: August 24, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To prepare teens for a meaningful service visit to Happy Life organization, teaching them about compassion, service, and how to connect with people from different life circumstances through Christ's love.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Compassion Connection"

Teens share stories of when they felt helped or showed kindness:

"A time when someone showed me unexpected kindness..."
"A moment I helped someone and how it made me feel..."
"What 'compassion' means to me personally..."

Discussion: "Today we'll learn how to extend Christ's love through our upcoming visit to Happy Life organization."

2. Biblical Foundations of Service (15 min)
15 min
A. God's Heart for the Vulnerable

Scriptural basis for serving others:

  • Pure religion cares for orphans and widows (James 1:27)
  • Jesus' ministry to the marginalized
  • We are Christ's hands and feet on earth
"Religion that God our Father accepts as pure and faultless is this: to look after orphans and widows in their distress..." James 1:27
B. Understanding Happy Life

About the organization we'll visit:

  • Mission: Providing care and hope to vulnerable children
  • Founded: 2002
  • Services: Residential care, education, family reunification
  • Core values: Compassion, dignity, empowerment
"And whoever welcomes one such child in my name welcomes me." Matthew 18:5
C. Heart Preparation

Developing the right attitude:

  • See people as God sees them
  • Listen more than speak
  • Offer presence rather than pity
  • Focus on building dignity
"Do nothing out of selfish ambition or vain conceit. Rather, in humility value others above yourselves..." Philippians 2:3
3. Visit Preparation (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Preparing Our Hearts and Hands"

What to Bring

Donations: Books, toys, school supplies

Handmade cards with encouraging messages

What to Wear

Comfortable, modest clothing

Closed-toe shoes for safety

Group t-shirts (provided)

How to Connect

Be present and engaged

Ask thoughtful questions

Share stories appropriately

Conversation Starters
What's your favorite game to play?
Tell me about your favorite subject in school
What makes you laugh the most?
If you could have any superpower, what would it be?
What's the best book you've ever read?
What are you really good at?
Pre-Visit Checklist
4. Reflection & Commitment (10 min)
10 min
Service Commitment

Post-Visit Commitment

Pray regularly for Happy Life
Share the experience with others
Follow up with letters or emails
Continue serving locally
"Each of you should use whatever gift you have received to serve others, as faithful stewards of God's grace in its various forms." 1 Peter 4:10

Closing Prayer: "Heavenly Father, thank you for the opportunity to serve at Happy Life. Prepare our hearts to be vessels of your love. Help us to see each person as you see them, to listen with compassion, and to serve with humility. May our visit bring joy, hope, and encouragement to everyone we meet. Guide our conversations and actions to reflect your grace. Amen."

Facilitator Tips
  • Assign teens specific roles during the visit (greeters, activity leaders, conversation starters)
  • Prepare simple crafts or games to do together
  • Discuss appropriate boundaries and behavior
  • Plan a debrief session after the visit
  • Follow up with thank you notes to Happy Life
