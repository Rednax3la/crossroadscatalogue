Redemption

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: March 2, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens understand the biblical concept of redemption, recognize Jesus Christ as their redeemer, and embrace the transformative power of being bought back from sin's slavery.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "The Pawn Shop"

Teens participate in a simulation of redeeming items:

"Bring 'valuable' personal items (phone, jewelry, etc.)"
"Facilitator acts as pawn shop owner setting redemption prices"
"Teens must pay the price to reclaim their items"

Discussion: "Just as you paid to reclaim your items, Jesus paid the ultimate price to redeem us from sin. Today we'll explore this powerful transaction."

2. The Redemption Story (15 min)
15 min
A. What is Redemption?

Biblical meaning of redemption:

  • Buying back from slavery (Leviticus 25:47-49)
  • Paying a ransom price (Mark 10:45)
  • Setting free from bondage
  • Restoring to original purpose
"For even the Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many." Mark 10:45
B. Our Need for Redemption

Why we needed to be bought back:

  • Enslaved to sin (John 8:34)
  • Spiritually bankrupt (Romans 3:23)
  • Under death's sentence (Romans 6:23)
  • Separated from God (Isaiah 59:2)
"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." Romans 3:23
C. The Redeemer's Price

What it cost to redeem us:

  • Christ's perfect life (Hebrews 4:15)
  • His sacrificial death (1 Peter 1:18-19)
  • Victory over the grave (1 Corinthians 15:55-57)
  • His ongoing intercession (Hebrews 7:25)
"For you know that it was not with perishable things such as silver or gold that you were redeemed... but with the precious blood of Christ." 1 Peter 1:18-19

4. Living Redeemed (10 min)
10 min
Freedom Declaration
The Redeemed Life

Characteristics of a redeemed person:

New Identity:

  • Child of God (John 1:12)
  • Citizen of heaven (Philippians 3:20)
  • Temple of the Spirit (1 Corinthians 6:19)
  • Royal priesthood (1 Peter 2:9)

New Purpose:

  • Worship the Redeemer
  • Walk in freedom
  • Witness to others
  • Work for God's kingdom

Weekly Challenge

Share your "Redemption Receipt" with one person this week and explain what being redeemed means to you.

"In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins, in accordance with the riches of God's grace." Ephesians 1:7

  • Create "redeemed" wristbands with Ephesians 1:7
  • Use play money to illustrate redemption price
