Life as a Gift

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: April 27, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens recognize life as a precious gift from God, cultivate gratitude for their existence, and understand their inherent value and purpose according to God's plan.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "The Best Gift Ever"

Teens share about meaningful gifts they've received:

What's the most memorable gift you've ever received?
What made that gift special?
How did you respond to the giver?

Transition: "Today we'll explore the greatest gift we've all received - the gift of life itself. How should we respond to the Giver of this gift?"

2. Understanding Life as a Gift (15 min)
15 min

Life: God's Precious Gift

"For you created my inmost being; you knit me together in my mother's womb. I praise you because I am fearfully and wonderfully made." Psalm 139:13-14
A. The Giver of Life
  • God as the source of all life (Genesis 2:7)
  • Each life intentionally created
  • We exist because God willed it
"The Spirit of God has made me; the breath of the Almighty gives me life." Job 33:4
B. The Value of Life
Inherent Worth

Not based on achievements

Unique Design

No one else like you

Eternal Significance

Created for eternity

C. The Purpose of Life

Why we've been given this gift:

  • To know and love God
  • To love and serve others
  • To care for creation
  • To fulfill God's unique plan
"For we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do." Ephesians 2:10

4. Living Gratefully (10 min)
10 min
Stewarding the Gift

30-Day Gift Challenge

Week 1: Notice Gifts
Week 2: Express Gratitude
Week 3: Share Life
Week 4: Honor the Giver
"I have come that they may have life, and have it to the full." John 10:10

Closing Prayer: "Gracious God, thank you for the incredible gift of life. Help us to cherish each day as a precious present from you. Teach us to live with gratitude, purpose, and joy. Show us how to honor you with the life you've given us. May we always remember that our worth comes from being your beloved children. Amen."

Facilitator Tips
  • Bring actual wrapped gifts as visual aids
  • Share stories of people who overcame challenges to appreciate life
  • Create a "Wall of Gratitude" with sticky notes
  • Provide journals for teens to record daily blessings
  • Discuss how to handle life's difficulties while maintaining gratitude
  • Emphasize that every life has divine purpose
