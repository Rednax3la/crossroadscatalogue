Conversations with God

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: March 9, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To teach teens how to develop authentic, two-way conversations with God through prayer, exploring different forms of prayer and learning to recognize God's voice in their daily lives.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Divine Text Messages"

Teens complete these conversation starters as if texting with God:

"God, today I'm feeling..."
"One thing I'm grateful for is..."
"I could really use your help with..."
"What do you want me to know today?"

Discussion: "Prayer is like an ongoing conversation with God. Today we'll learn how to make it authentic and two-way."

2. Elements of Prayer (15 min)
15 min
A. Prayer as Relationship

Understanding prayer as conversation:

  • Not a religious ritual but relational connection
  • God desires authentic communication (Jeremiah 29:12)
  • Prayer includes both speaking and listening
  • Consistency over eloquence
"Then you will call on me and come and pray to me, and I will listen to you." Jeremiah 29:12
B. Types of Prayer

Different forms of conversation with God:

Adoration (Worship)
Confession
Thanksgiving
Supplication (Asking)
"Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God." Philippians 4:6
C. Recognizing God's Voice

How God speaks to us:

  • Through Scripture (2 Timothy 3:16)
  • Through inner promptings (John 10:27)
  • Through circumstances
  • Through godly counsel
  • Through peace and conviction
"My sheep listen to my voice; I know them, and they follow me." John 10:27
3. Prayer Practice (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Prayer Phone Simulator"
Hi [Name], I'm always here for you. What's on your heart today?
God, I'm worried about my test tomorrow.
I know school feels overwhelming. Remember I'm with you. Have you studied?
Yes, but I'm still nervous.
Trust me. I'll give you peace as you trust in me. Philippians 4:6-7

Interactive Exercise: Teens practice two-way prayer by "texting" with God, learning to express themselves honestly and listen for responses.

God's Voice

World's Noise

Our Distractions

Listening Exercise: Guide teens through a 5-minute silent listening prayer, encouraging them to quiet other noises and focus on God's voice.

4. Prayer Commitment (10 min)
10 min
Daily Prayer Challenge

7-Day Prayer Challenge

Day 1: Adoration
Day 2: Confession
Day 3: Thanksgiving
Day 4: Supplication
Day 5: Listening
Day 6: Scripture
Day 7: All Elements
"Rejoice always, pray continually, give thanks in all circumstances; for this is God's will for you in Christ Jesus." 1 Thessalonians 5:16-18

Closing Prayer: "Father, thank you for inviting us into conversation with you. Help us to approach prayer not as a duty but as a delight. Teach us to speak honestly, listen attentively, and recognize your voice in our daily lives. May our prayer lives grow deeper and more authentic each day. Amen."

Bonus Tips for Facilitators
  • Share your personal prayer journey with teens
  • Create a prayer wall with sticky notes
  • Use the ACTS model (Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication)
  • Provide prayer journals with prompts
  • Lead guided prayer exercises with soft background music
