My Servant or Master?

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: June 15, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens understand the dangers of materialism and learn to develop a healthy relationship with money and possessions, recognizing that we can only serve one master - God or wealth.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "What Would You Do?"

Teens discuss these scenarios:

"If you found $100, what would you do with it?"
"What's one thing you really want but can't afford?"
"How much money would make you feel 'rich'?"
"What's more important - time or money?"

Discussion: "Our relationship with money reveals who we're really serving. Today we'll explore how to keep money as our servant rather than becoming its slave."

2. Materialism vs. Contentment (15 min)
15 min
A. The Danger of Materialism

Why Jesus warned about wealth:

  • Money promises security but can't deliver
  • Possessions demand our time and attention
  • Wealth creates false sense of independence
  • Material things distract from eternal values
"Then Jesus said to them, 'Watch out! Be on your guard against all kinds of greed; life does not consist in an abundance of possessions.'" Luke 12:15
B. Two Masters

Why we can't serve both God and money:

"No one can serve two masters. Either you will hate the one and love the other, or you will be devoted to the one and despise the other. You cannot serve both God and money." Matthew 6:24
C. True Riches

What God values most:

Relationships
Generosity
Faith
Character
"But store up for yourselves treasures in heaven, where moths and vermin do not destroy, and where thieves do not break in and steal." Matthew 6:20
3. Money Mastery (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Priority Check"
Buying the latest smartphone
Tithing to my church
Saving for college
Buying designer clothes
Giving to someone in need
Saving for a car

Discussion: Drag to reorder these financial priorities according to what you think honors God. How do your choices reflect who you're serving?

Generosity Challenge
Give anonymously to someone
Buy lunch for a friend
Donate to a charity
Volunteer your time

Challenge: Choose one generosity action to complete this week.

4. Contentment Commitment (10 min)
10 min
Freedom from Materialism

7-Day Contentment Challenge

Day 1: Avoid shopping
Day 2: Donate something
Day 3: Practice gratitude
Day 4: Fast from social media
Day 5: Serve someone
Day 6: Spend time in nature
Day 7: Give generously
"Keep your lives free from the love of money and be content with what you have, because God has said, 'Never will I leave you; never will I forsake you.'" Hebrews 13:5
"Command those who are rich in this present world not to be arrogant nor to put their hope in wealth, which is so uncertain, but to put their hope in God, who richly provides us with everything for our enjoyment." 1 Timothy 6:17

Closing Prayer: "Lord, help us to see money and possessions as tools to serve you rather than masters to serve. Free us from the trap of materialism and teach us contentment. Show us how to be generous with what you've given us, storing up treasures in heaven rather than on earth. Amen."

Practical Tips
  • Practice the 24-hour rule before non-essential purchases
  • Create a gratitude journal for what you already have
  • Set a generosity budget (10% is a good starting point)
  • Regularly evaluate what you own and donate unused items
  • Limit exposure to advertising and social media that promotes materialism
