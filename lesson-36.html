Effective Communication

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: September 7, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To teach teens biblical principles of effective communication, including active listening, respectful speaking, and conflict resolution, to build healthier relationships with family, friends, and God.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Telephone Challenge"

Teens play a game of telephone with a biblical message:

"Love is patient, love is kind"
"Speak the truth in love"
"Be quick to listen, slow to speak"

Discussion: "Communication breakdowns happen easily. Today we'll learn how to communicate clearly and effectively from a biblical perspective."

2. Biblical Communication (15 min)
15 min
A. God's Design for Communication

Biblical foundations:

  • Words have power to build up or tear down (Prov 18:21)
  • Speaking truth in love (Eph 4:15)
  • Gentle answers turn away wrath (Prov 15:1)
  • Being quick to listen, slow to speak (James 1:19)
"The tongue has the power of life and death." Proverbs 18:21
B. Communication Styles

Different ways people communicate:

Aggressive
Passive
Passive-Aggressive
Assertive
"Do not let any unwholesome talk come out of your mouths, but only what is helpful for building others up." Ephesians 4:29
3. Communication Skills Lab (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Active Listening Practice"
1
Give full attention (put devices away)
2
Show you're listening (nod, eye contact)
3
Reflect what you heard ("So you're saying...")
4
Ask clarifying questions
5
Respond appropriately
"I" Statements Practice
"I feel frustrated when chores aren't shared, because I feel like I'm doing all the work."
"I feel hurt when plans change last minute, because I was looking forward to spending time together."
"I feel proud when I finish a project, because I worked hard on it."

Communication Role-Play

Practice assertive communication with common scenarios:

  • Asking for help with schoolwork
  • Expressing hurt feelings to a friend
  • Disagreeing respectfully with parents
4. Conflict Resolution (10 min)
10 min
Peaceful Resolution Skills

Pause

Take a break if emotions are high

Pray

Seek God's wisdom and perspective

Perspective

Try to see the other person's viewpoint

Peace

Seek reconciliation and understanding

"If it is possible, as far as it depends on you, live at peace with everyone." Romans 12:18

Closing Prayer: "Lord, thank you for the gift of communication. Help us to use our words to build up and not tear down. Teach us to listen with compassion, speak with kindness, and resolve conflicts with grace. May our communication reflect your love and wisdom in all our relationships. Amen."

Communication Tips
  • Think before you speak - is it true, kind, necessary?
  • Use "I" statements instead of "you" accusations
  • Practice active listening without interrupting
  • Pay attention to non-verbal cues
  • Seek to understand before being understood
