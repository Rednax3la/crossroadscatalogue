The Fall of Man

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: February 23, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens understand the biblical account of humanity's fall, recognize the nature and consequences of sin, and discover God's redemptive plan that begins immediately after the fall.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Temptation Chain Reaction"

Teens participate in a domino effect game illustrating how one choice affects many:

"Set up dominoes in a circle. Removing one piece causes the whole circle to collapse."
"Discussion: How do our choices affect others in our family, school, or community?"

Teaching Point: "Just as one domino affects the whole chain, Adam and Eve's choice affected all humanity. Today we'll explore the first sin and its consequences."

2. The Fall Narrative (15 min)
15 min
A. The Perfect Garden (Genesis 2)

God's original design for humanity:

  • Perfect relationship with God
  • Harmony with creation
  • Meaningful work without toil
  • Transparent relationships
"God saw all that he had made, and it was very good." Genesis 1:31
B. The Serpent's Deception (Genesis 3:1-5)

Tactics of temptation:

  • Questioning God's command (v.1)
  • Denying God's goodness (v.4)
  • Promising false benefits (v.5)
  • Appealing to pride and desire
"You will not certainly die," the serpent said to the woman. "For God knows that when you eat from it your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God..." Genesis 3:4-5
C. The Choice and Consequences (Genesis 3:6-24)

Immediate results of disobedience:

  • Broken relationship with God
  • Shame and hiding
  • Cursed creation
  • Pain in work and relationships
  • Physical death introduced
"When the woman saw that the fruit of the tree was good for food and pleasing to the eye, and also desirable for gaining wisdom, she took some and ate it." Genesis 3:6
3. Temptation & Consequences (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Modern Temptation Scales"
Truth
"Everyone's doing it"
"It's not that bad"
"You deserve this"
Lie
"No one will know"
"God wants you happy"
"This will solve your problems"

Exercise: Teens take turns placing temptation cards on either side of the scale, observing how lies often outweigh truth when we're vulnerable.

Consequences Then and Now

Original Consequences:

  • Separation from God
  • Pain in childbirth
  • Toilsome work
  • Relational conflict
  • Physical death

Modern Parallels:

  • Spiritual emptiness
  • Broken families
  • Work-life imbalance
  • Social media conflict
  • Existential anxiety
4. Redemption & Closing (10 min)
10 min
The First Gospel
Genesis 3:15 - The Protoevangelium

"And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and hers; he will crush your head, and you will strike his heel."

Symbolism:

  • Serpent = Satan
  • Woman's offspring = Christ
  • Crushed head = Satan's defeat
  • Struck heel = Christ's sacrifice

Hope in Darkness:

  • God's plan begins immediately
  • Promise of a Savior
  • Ultimate victory assured
  • Grace after judgment

Weekly Challenge

Identify one area where you face temptation and create a "truth response" based on God's Word to counter the enemy's lies.

Closing Prayer: "Heavenly Father, we acknowledge the seriousness of sin and its consequences. Thank you for providing redemption through Jesus even in the midst of judgment. Help us recognize temptation and cling to your truth. May we find hope in your promise to crush the serpent's head through Christ. Amen."

Bonus Tips for Facilitators
  • Create a "Garden of Eden" corner with plants and fabric
  • Use visual props: plastic fruit, serpent toy
  • Show artwork depicting the fall through history
  • Share personal stories of overcoming temptation
  • Provide cards with truth statements to combat lies
