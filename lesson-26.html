Taking Initiative

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: June 29, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To empower teens to recognize opportunities and take godly initiative in their daily lives, developing the confidence to step forward and make a difference without waiting for others.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "First Steps"

Teens share experiences of taking initiative:

"A time I saw a need and stepped up to help..."
"Something I started without being asked..."
"A project I took the lead on..."
"When I solved a problem before it got worse..."

Discussion: "Taking initiative means seeing what needs to be done and doing it without waiting for instructions. Today we'll learn how to develop this important life skill."

2. Biblical Initiative (15 min)
15 min
A. What is Initiative?

More than just taking action:

  • Seeing opportunities others miss
  • Taking responsibility without being asked
  • Making things happen rather than waiting
  • Solving problems proactively
"Do you see someone skilled in their work? They will serve before kings; they will not serve before officials of low rank." Proverbs 22:29

3. Initiative Practice (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Opportunity Spotting"
Home: You notice the kitchen is always messy after dinner
School: Your classmate is struggling with an assignment
Church: The youth room needs organizing
Community: The local park has litter accumulating

Exercise: For each opportunity, brainstorm specific actions you could take without being asked.

4. Initiative Challenge (10 min)
10 min
Take the Initiative Challenge

7-Day Initiative Challenge

Day 1: Do an unasked chore
Day 2: Help a classmate
Day 3: Solve a problem
Day 4: Start a positive project
Day 5: Speak encouragement
Day 6: Organize something
Day 7: Lead a small effort
"Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters." Colossians 3:23

Closing Prayer: "Lord, thank you for creating us to make a difference in this world. Give us eyes to see opportunities where we can take initiative. Remove our fears and hesitations, and fill us with your wisdom and courage. Help us to step forward in faith, knowing you go before us. Amen."

Initiative Boosters
  • Ask "What needs to be done?" in every situation
  • Practice small acts of initiative daily
  • Find a role model who demonstrates initiative
  • Don't wait for perfect conditions - start now
  • Celebrate when you take initiative, regardless of outcome
