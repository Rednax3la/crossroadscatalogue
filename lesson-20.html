Habit Formation & Resilience

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: May 18, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To teach teens how to build positive habits and develop resilience, helping them understand the science of habit formation and the biblical principles of perseverance through challenges.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Habit Walk"

Teens walk along a path while discussing:

What's one habit you do without thinking?
What habit would you like to develop?
How do you react when facing obstacles?

Transition: "Today we'll learn how to intentionally build good habits and develop resilience to overcome challenges."

2. The Science of Habits (15 min)
15 min
A. Habit Loop
  • Cue: Trigger for the behavior
  • Routine: The behavior itself
  • Reward: Benefit from the behavior
"I can do all this through him who gives me strength." Philippians 4:13
B. Building New Habits

Effective strategies:

  • Start small (2-minute rule)
  • Stack habits (pair new with existing)
  • Design your environment
  • Track your progress
"Do not despise these small beginnings, for the LORD rejoices to see the work begin." Zechariah 4:10 (NLT)
C. Breaking Bad Habits

Keys to change:

  • Identify triggers and rewards
  • Replace rather than eliminate
  • Change your environment
  • Practice self-compassion

Closing Prayer: "Lord, thank you for designing us with the capacity to grow and change. Help us to build habits that honor you and develop resilience through life's challenges. Give us wisdom to form good routines, courage to face difficulties, and faith to trust in your strength when we feel weak. May our lives reflect your transforming power. Amen."

Facilitator Tips
  • Share your personal habit-formation journey
  • Discuss times you've needed resilience
  • Provide habit-tracking printables
  • Introduce the "Don't Break the Chain" method
  • Suggest habit apps: Habitica, Streaks, Loop
  • Follow up next week to check on habit progress
