Spiritual Journaling

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: March 16, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To teach teens the value of journaling as a tool for spiritual growth, self-reflection, and prayer, providing practical methods to develop this transformative habit.
1. Opening Icebreaker (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Finish the Sentence"

Teens complete prompts aloud or write responses:

"Today, I feel..."
"Something God has been teaching me lately is..."
"One thing I'm grateful for is..."
"A challenge I'm facing right now is..."

Discussion: "Journaling helps us process thoughts and emotions, just like this activity did. Today we'll learn how to make this a regular spiritual practice."

2. The Why and How of Journaling (15 min)
15 min
A. What is Spiritual Journaling?

More than just diary writing:

  • Recording prayers and God's answers
  • Reflecting on Scripture
  • Processing life experiences through faith
  • Tracking spiritual growth
"I will remember the deeds of the LORD; yes, I will remember your miracles of long ago." Psalm 77:11
B. Why Journal?

Benefits of a consistent journaling practice:

Deepens Prayer Life
Clarifies Thoughts
Tracks Spiritual Growth
Preserves God's Faithfulness
"But Mary treasured up all these things and pondered them in her heart." Luke 2:19
C. Biblical Foundations

Scriptural basis for journaling:

  • David's psalms as emotional journals
  • Jeremiah's laments
  • Paul's reflective letters
  • Jesus' encouragement to remember
"Write down the revelation and make it plain on tablets so that a herald may run with it." Habakkuk 2:2
3. Journaling Practice (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Guided Journaling"
Dear God, Today I want to share...
"Letter to God"
"Scripture Reflection"
"Gratitude List"
"Challenge Processing"
Journal Decoration

Facilitator Tip: Encourage teens to add symbols, drawings, or colors to express what words cannot capture.

4. Commitment & Closing (10 min)
10 min
Journaling Commitment

Weekly Journaling Challenge

Commit to journaling at least 3 times this week using these methods:

Prayer Journaling
Scripture Reflection
Gratitude Journaling
"These things happened to them as examples and were written down as warnings for us." 1 Corinthians 10:11

Closing Prayer: "Lord, thank you for the gift of journaling as a way to grow closer to you. Help us to be faithful in recording our journey with you. May our journals become treasured records of your faithfulness, guidance, and transformation in our lives. Amen."

Takeaway Tip

Start small! Even 5 minutes of journaling daily can transform your spiritual life over time.

Bonus Tips for Facilitators
  • Share excerpts from your own spiritual journal (appropriate ones)
  • Provide small notebooks or journaling templates
  • Suggest journaling apps: Day One, YouVersion Journal, Penzu
  • Create a "Journaling Station" with art supplies
  • Recommend the SOAP method (Scripture, Observation, Application, Prayer)
