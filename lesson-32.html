Bike Riding & Fun Park Hangout

Duration: 4 hours
Date: August 10, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Location: Riverside Park
Objective: To facilitate a fun, safe outdoor fellowship activity that promotes teamwork, physical activity, and spiritual connection through shared experiences in God's creation.
1. Event Overview
10 min
Welcome & Introduction

Start with prayer and explain the day's purpose: "Today we're combining adventure with fellowship to experience God's creation together. We'll challenge ourselves physically while supporting each other as a community."

Spiritual Theme

"Running the Race with Perseverance" (Hebrews 12:1-2) - We'll focus on endurance, encouragement, and keeping our eyes on Jesus throughout our activities.

Activity Goals
  • Build community through shared adventure
  • Encourage physical activity and stewardship of our bodies
  • Create space for spiritual conversations in nature
  • Develop teamwork and encouragement skills
2. Event Schedule
4 hours
Timeline of Activities
1:00 PM - Arrival & Group Assignment

Check-in, safety briefing, and division into small groups of 5-6 teens with 1 leader each.

1:30 PM - Bike Riding Adventure

Guided 5-mile trail ride along the river with designated rest stops. Groups stay together throughout.

3:00 PM - Fun Park Activities

Groups rotate through stations: mini-golf, climbing wall, and team challenge course.

4:00 PM - Reflection & Snacks

Groups gather for discussion and refreshments at the picnic area.

4:45 PM - Closing & Prayer

Final gathering, share experiences, and closing prayer.

5:00 PM - Pickup

Parents arrive for pickup at the main entrance.

Map of Riverside Park with activity locations

3. Safety & Preparation
15 min
Safety Guidelines
Mandatory Helmets

Provided for all biking activities

Buddy System

Stay with your assigned partner

Sun Protection

Sunscreen and water provided

First Aid

Leaders carry first aid kits

What to Bring Checklist
"For God is not a God of disorder but of peace." 1 Corinthians 14:33
4. Spiritual Connection Points
30 min
Discussion Prompts for Group Leaders
During Bike Ride: "As we ride through this beautiful scenery, what does it reveal to you about God's character?"
At Rest Stop: "The Christian life is sometimes called a 'race.' What helps you keep going when things get difficult?"
At Challenge Course: "How did you encourage teammates today? How does this relate to our calling to 'carry each other's burdens'?"
During Snack Time: "Share a time when you felt God's presence during an ordinary activity like we're doing today."
Devotional Focus

Hebrews 12:1-2 (NIV): "Therefore, since we are surrounded by such a great cloud of witnesses, let us throw off everything that hinders and the sin that so easily entangles. And let us run with perseverance the race marked out for us, fixing our eyes on Jesus, the pioneer and perfecter of faith."

Discussion Questions:

  • What might be "hindering" or "entangling" you in your spiritual journey?
  • How can we help each other "run with perseverance"?
  • What does it mean to "fix our eyes on Jesus" in daily life?
5. Closing & Logistics
15 min
Facilitator Responsibilities
Emergency Procedures
  • Minor Injury: Administer first aid and notify program director
  • Serious Injury: Call 911 first, then notify parents and director
  • Missing Participant: Immediately notify all leaders, search assigned areas, contact authorities if not found in 10 minutes
  • Weather Emergency: Move to designated shelter areas immediately

Parent Communication

Email sent to parents one week prior with:

  • Detailed schedule and location information
  • Required permission forms
  • What to wear and bring
  • Pickup/dropoff procedures
  • Emergency contact information
