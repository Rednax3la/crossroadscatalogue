Spiritual Fasting

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: May 4, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To teach teens the biblical purpose and practice of fasting as a spiritual discipline, helping them understand how to incorporate fasting into their relationship with God in age-appropriate ways.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Hunger Awareness"

Teens discuss these questions in small groups:

What's the longest you've gone without food? How did you feel?
What helps you when you feel really hungry?
How might physical hunger relate to spiritual hunger?

Transition: "Today we'll explore fasting - intentionally going without food to focus on spiritual hunger and deepen our relationship with God."

2. Understanding Fasting (15 min)
15 min
A. What is Biblical Fasting?
  • Voluntarily abstaining from food for spiritual purposes
  • Focusing on God rather than physical needs
  • Seeking God's guidance, help, or presence
  • Expressing repentance or dependence on God
"When you fast, do not look somber as the hypocrites do, for they disfigure their faces to show others they are fasting. Truly I tell you, they have received their reward in full." Matthew 6:16
B. Types of Fasts
Partial Fast

Abstaining from certain foods

Sunrise to Sunset

Daylight hours only

24-Hour Fast

One full day

Media Fast

Abstaining from technology

C. Why Fast?

Biblical purposes for fasting:

  • To draw near to God (James 4:8)
  • To seek guidance in decision-making
  • To express repentance
  • To intercede for others
  • To prepare for ministry
"While they were worshiping the Lord and fasting, the Holy Spirit said, 'Set apart for me Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them.'" Acts 13:2
3. Practical Fasting (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Fasting Journey"
Fasting Progress
Beginner Challenge: "Sunrise to Sunset"

Fast from breakfast to dinner, replacing meal times with prayer and Scripture reading.

Intermediate Challenge: "Media Fast"

Abstain from social media/video games for 24 hours, using that time for spiritual activities.

Fasting Replacement Plan
Meal Time → Prayer Time
Screen Time → Bible Time
Complaining → Thankfulness
Shopping → Serving
"Is not this the kind of fasting I have chosen: to loose the chains of injustice and untie the cords of the yoke, to set the oppressed free and break every yoke?" Isaiah 58:6
4. Fasting Challenge (10 min)
10 min
7-Day Fasting Challenge

Fasting Progression Plan

Day 1: 1 Meal
Day 2: Social Media
Day 3: Sweets
Day 4: Video Games
Day 5: Breakfast
Day 6: Lunch
Day 7: Sunrise-Sunset
"But when you fast, put oil on your head and wash your face, so that it will not be obvious to others that you are fasting, but only to your Father, who is unseen; and your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you." Matthew 6:17-18

Closing Prayer: "Father, thank you for the spiritual discipline of fasting. As we explore this practice, help us to seek you with all our hearts. Give us wisdom to fast appropriately and safely. May our times of fasting draw us closer to you and align our hearts with your purposes. Amen."

Facilitator Tips
  • Emphasize fasting as a spiritual practice, not a diet
  • Discuss medical considerations - consult parents if needed
  • Suggest alternatives for those who can't fast from food
  • Provide journal prompts for fasting times
  • Share your own fasting experiences
  • Follow up next week to discuss experiences
