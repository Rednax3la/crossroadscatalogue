Introduction to Crossroads

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: January 5, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To introduce teens to the Crossroads program as a transformative journey of faith, helping them understand its purpose and their role within this spiritual adventure.
1. Opening Icebreaker (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Spiritual Road Trip"

Begin with an engaging activity that helps teens visualize their faith journey:

"If your faith journey was a road trip, what kind of vehicle would you be in and why?"
"Who would be your travel companions on this spiritual adventure?"
"What's one destination you hope to reach in your relationship with God this year?"

Discussion: Explain that just like a road trip, our faith journey has a starting point, destinations, and companions. Crossroads is designed to be the roadmap for this adventure.

2. Understanding Crossroads (15 min)
15 min
A. What is Crossroads?

A year-long spiritual journey designed specifically for teens to deepen their faith, build authentic relationships, and discover their God-given purpose.

"Trust in the LORD with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight." Proverbs 3:5-6
B. Why a Journey Metaphor?

Faith is not a destination but a lifelong adventure. Crossroads provides:

  • A roadmap for spiritual growth
  • Companions for the journey
  • Tools for navigating challenges
"Two are better than one... If either of them falls down, one can help the other up." Ecclesiastes 4:9-10
C. Our Guiding Principles

Three foundational pillars of Crossroads:

  1. Discover: Encountering God personally
  2. Grow: Developing Christ-like character
  3. Impact: Making a difference in the world
"Being confident of this, that he who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion." Philippians 1:6
3. Interactive Activity (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Faith Journey Mapping"

Guide teens in creating a visual representation of their spiritual journey:

A. Roadmap Creation

Provide large paper and art supplies. Have teens draw:

  • Where they started their faith journey
  • Key milestones they've passed
  • Current location on the road
  • Future destinations they hope to reach
B. Crossroads Signposts

Have them mark significant "crossroads" moments:

  • Important decisions they've made
  • Challenges they've overcome
  • People who influenced their journey

Facilitator Note: Walk around and engage with each teen, asking open-ended questions about their maps. Share parts of your own faith journey to model vulnerability.

4. Commitment & Closing (10 min)
10 min
Group Commitment

Create a "Crossroads Covenant" that everyone signs:

  • I commit to attending regularly
  • I will participate with an open heart
  • I promise to respect others' journeys
  • I will bring my Bible, Notebook and Pen each week

Weekly Challenge

Share your faith journey map with one person this week and explain what you hope to gain from Crossroads.

"Let us run with perseverance the race marked out for us, fixing our eyes on Jesus, the pioneer and perfecter of faith." Hebrews 12:1-2

Closing Prayer: "Lord, as we begin this Crossroads journey together, open our hearts to your guidance. Give us courage to navigate the twists and turns of faith, and help us grow closer to you each step of the way. Amen."

Bonus Tips for Facilitators
  • Share a personal story of a "crossroads moment" in your own faith journey
  • Bring a physical road map as a visual aid
  • Play background music like "Where the Road Leads" by Point of Grace during the mapping activity
  • Provide high-quality art supplies to make the mapping activity engaging
