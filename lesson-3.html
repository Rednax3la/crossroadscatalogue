Journey Through Life

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: January 19, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens understand life as a spiritual journey guided by God, exploring how to navigate challenges and recognize divine direction through faith transitions.
1. Opening Icebreaker (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Roadblock Charades"

Teens act out common life challenges without speaking while others guess:

"Navigating friendship conflicts"
"Dealing with school pressure"
"Facing family changes"
"Making tough moral decisions"

Discussion: "Just as roads have obstacles, our spiritual journey has challenges. Today we'll learn how God guides us through them."

2. The Faith Journey (15 min)
15 min
A. God as Our Guide

Understanding God's role in our journey:

  • He knows the path ahead (Psalm 139:1-4)
  • He provides direction (Proverbs 3:5-6)
  • He walks with us (Deuteronomy 31:8)
"The LORD himself goes before you and will be with you; he will never leave you nor forsake you." Deuteronomy 31:8
B. Biblical Journey Examples

Learning from scriptural journeys:

  • Abraham's journey of faith (Genesis 12:1-4)
  • Israel's wilderness experience (Exodus 13:21-22)
  • Ruth's journey of loyalty (Ruth 1:16-17)
  • The disciples' journey with Jesus
"By faith Abraham... obeyed and went, even though he did not know where he was going." Hebrews 11:8
C. Journey Markers

Recognizing significant moments:

  • Divine appointments
  • Answered prayers
  • Growth through trials
  • Spiritual milestones
"I will remember the deeds of the LORD; yes, I will remember your miracles of long ago." Psalm 77:11
3. Journey Mapping (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Faith Road Atlas"

Guide teens in creating a personal spiritual journey map:

A. Mapping Your Journey

Create a timeline with significant events:

  • Spiritual birthplace
  • Mountaintop experiences
  • Valley moments
  • Divine detours
B. Divine GPS

Identify God's guidance at key points:

  • Scriptures that guided you
  • People who helped you
  • Prayers that sustained you
C. Future Destinations

Chart desired spiritual destinations:

  • Character qualities to develop
  • Service opportunities
  • Faith goals

Facilitator Note: Provide road-themed templates, stickers, and markers. Play "I Will Follow" by Chris Tomlin during the activity.

4. Commissioning & Closing (10 min)
10 min
Spiritual Compass Dedication

Guide teens in creating a "spiritual compass" with four directions:

North: God's Word

"Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path." (Psalm 119:105)

South: Prayer

"Pray continually." (1 Thessalonians 5:17)

East: Community

"As iron sharpens iron..." (Proverbs 27:17)

West: Worship

"Worship the LORD with gladness." (Psalm 100:2)

Weekly Challenge

Use your spiritual compass daily: Each morning, choose one direction to focus on that day and journal about how it guided you.

"Show me your ways, LORD, teach me your paths. Guide me in your truth and teach me, for you are God my Savior, and my hope is in you all day long." Psalm 25:4-5

Closing Prayer: "Heavenly Guide, thank you for walking with us on this journey. Help us trust your direction when the path is unclear. Give us courage to face challenges and wisdom to follow your compass. May we journey faithfully with you each day. Amen."

Bonus Tips for Facilitators
  • Share a personal "journey story" with photos from your own faith journey
  • Create a large floor map for teens to walk through during discussion
  • Use road signs as visual metaphors (Yield, Stop, Detour, etc.)
  • Provide compass keychains as take-home reminders
  • Set up a prayer "rest stop" station for quiet reflection
