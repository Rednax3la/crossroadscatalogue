Journey Through Life

January 25, 2026 50 minutes Ages 13–14 Lesson 3

Just as roads have obstacles, our spiritual journey has challenges — today we learn how God guides us through them.

10 min

Section 1: Opening Icebreaker

Activity: “Roadblock Charades”

Teens act out common life challenges without speaking while others guess.

“Navigating friendship conflicts”
“Dealing with school pressure”
“Facing family changes”
“Making tough moral decisions”

“Just as roads have obstacles, our spiritual journey has challenges. Today we will learn how God guides us through them.”

15 min

Section 2: The Faith Journey

A. God as Our Guide
  • He knows the path ahead (Psalm 139:1–4)
  • He provides direction (Proverbs 3:5–6)
  • He walks with us (Deuteronomy 31:8)
“The LORD himself goes before you and will be with you; he will never leave you nor forsake you.”
Deuteronomy 31:8
B. Biblical Journey Examples
  • Abraham’s journey (Genesis 12:1–4)
  • Israel’s wilderness experience (Exodus 13:21–22)
  • Ruth’s journey of loyalty (Ruth 1:16–17)
  • The disciples’ journey with Jesus
“By faith Abraham… obeyed and went, even though he did not know where he was going.”
Hebrews 11:8
C. Journey Markers
  • Divine appointments
  • Answered prayers
  • Growth through trials
  • Spiritual milestones
“I will remember the deeds of the LORD; yes, I will remember your miracles of long ago.”
Psalm 77:11
20 min

Section 3: Journey Mapping

Activity: “Faith Road Atlas”
Point A: Mapping Your Journey

Create a timeline of your spiritual story:

  • Spiritual birthplace
  • Mountaintop experiences
  • Valley moments
  • Divine detours
Point B: Divine GPS

Identify God’s guidance in your journey:

  • Scriptures that guided you
  • People who helped
  • Prayers that sustained you
Point C: Future Destinations

Chart desired spiritual destinations:

  • Character qualities to develop
  • Service opportunities
  • Faith goals
Facilitator Note

Provide road-themed templates, stickers, and markers. Play “I Will Follow” by Chris Tomlin during the activity.

10 min

Section 4: Commissioning & Closing

Activity: Spiritual Compass Dedication

Guide teens in creating a “spiritual compass” with four directions:

North — God’s Word
“Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path.”
Psalm 119:105
South — Prayer
“Pray continually.”
1 Thessalonians 5:17
East — Community
“As iron sharpens iron…”
Proverbs 27:17
West — Worship
“Worship the LORD with gladness.”
Psalm 100:2
Weekly Challenge

Use your spiritual compass daily: each morning choose one direction to focus on and journal about how it guided you.

Scripture
“Show me your ways, LORD, teach me your paths. Guide me in your truth and teach me, for you are God my Savior, and my hope is in you all day long.”
Psalm 25:4–5
Closing Prayer

“Heavenly Guide, thank you for walking with us on this journey. Help us trust your direction when the path is unclear. Give us courage to face challenges and wisdom to follow your compass. May we journey faithfully with you each day. Amen.”

Facilitator Tips
  • Share personal journey story with photos
  • Create a large floor map for the group
  • Use road signs as visual metaphors (Yield, Stop, Detour)
  • Provide compass keychains as take-home items
  • Set up a prayer rest stop station
