Camp: Spiritual Growth & Relationships

Duration: 90 minutes
Date: November 23, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Special Camp Session
Objective: To provide campers with practical tools for spiritual growth through Quiet Time and journaling, while fostering healthy relationships through guided conversations in a safe camp environment.
1. How to Have Meaningful Quiet Time (QT)
Understanding Quiet Time
"Be still, and know that I am God; I will be exalted among the nations, I will be exalted in the earth." Psalm 46:10
What is QT?
  • Dedicated time alone with God
  • Focus on prayer and Scripture
  • Listening more than speaking
  • Creating space for reflection
The SOAP Method
  • Scripture (Read a passage)
  • Observation (What stands out?)
  • Application (How can I apply this?)
  • Prayer (Talk to God about it)

Camp QT Challenge

Commit to 15 minutes of quiet time each morning during camp:

Find a quiet spot in nature
Read one Psalm daily
Journal your reflections
Pray for someone specific
2. Saunter & Journaling in Nature
The Art of Sauntering

Sauntering is walking with a reflective, unhurried pace - observing God's creation and listening for His voice.

"The heavens declare the glory of God; the skies proclaim the work of his hands." Psalm 19:1

Observation Walk

Notice 5 things you've never seen before in nature

Prayer Walk

Pray for people/circumstances as you walk

Question Walk

Ask God one question and listen for answers

Camp Journaling Guide
Today in nature, I noticed...
Something God spoke to me during quiet time...
A challenge I'm facing and how God might be working through it...
People I'm praying for this week...

Nature Journaling Challenge

During camp, combine sauntering with journaling:

  1. Take a 20-minute saunter after breakfast
  2. Find a quiet spot in nature
  3. Spend 10 minutes journaling your observations
  4. Write one prayer based on what you experienced
3. Creating Positive Relationships (CPR)
Building Healthy Connections
"Do to others as you would have them do to you." Luke 6:31

Communication

Active listening, honest expression

Perspective

Seeing from others' viewpoints

Respect

Honoring boundaries and differences

Couch Conversations

Guided Opposite-Sex Conversations

In a safe camp setting, facilitators will guide conversations between guys and girls to build healthy understanding and communication skills.

What does true friendship mean to you?
How can we create a supportive community?
What are appropriate boundaries in friendships?

Conversation Guidelines

Listen without judgment
Respect different views
Maintain appropriate boundaries
Speak honestly but kindly
CPR Commitment

Relationship Challenge

During camp, practice CPR in all relationships:

Initiate one meaningful conversation daily
Practice active listening
Respect personal boundaries
Pray for your relationships
