Decision Making

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: September 21, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To equip teens with biblical decision-making principles and practical tools to make wise choices that honor God in all areas of life.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Pathways Game"

Teens choose paths for various scenarios:

"You find $50 in the hallway at school..."
"Your friends want you to try something you're uncomfortable with..."
"You have to choose between church event and a friend's party..."

Discussion: "Every decision leads us down a path. Today we'll learn how to choose wisely."

2. Biblical Decision Making (15 min)
15 min
A. God's Guidance in Decisions

How God directs our paths:

  • Through Scripture (Psalm 119:105)
  • Through prayer and discernment (James 1:5)
  • Through wise counsel (Proverbs 15:22)
  • Through the Holy Spirit (John 16:13)
"Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path." Psalm 119:105
B. Decision-Making Factors

Key considerations for choices:

Scripture
Prayer
Wise Counsel
Core Values
"Trust in the LORD with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding." Proverbs 3:5
3. Decision Practice (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "Decision Workshop"
Decision-Making Model
1
Identify the decision to be made
2
Gather relevant information
3
Identify alternatives
4
Weigh the evidence
5
Choose among alternatives
6
Take action
7
Review your decision
Scenario Practice
Academic Choice

Choosing between advanced classes and more free time

Friendship Dilemma

How to respond when a friend shares someone else's secret

Time Management

Balancing church, school, family, and personal time

Decision Challenge

Apply the 7-step model to one real-life decision this week

4. Wisdom Commitment (10 min)
10 min
Commitment to Wise Choices

My Decision-Making Commitment

I commit to seeking God's wisdom in all my decisions. I will:

  • Consult Scripture for guidance
  • Pray before making important choices
  • Seek counsel from wise mentors
  • Consider how decisions honor God
  • Take responsibility for my choices
"If any of you lacks wisdom, you should ask God, who gives generously to all without finding fault, and it will be given to you." James 1:5

Closing Prayer: "Heavenly Father, thank you for promising wisdom to those who ask. Guide us in our decision-making processes. Help us to seek your will above our own desires. Give us discernment to recognize your path and courage to follow it. May our choices bring honor to you and blessing to others. Amen."
