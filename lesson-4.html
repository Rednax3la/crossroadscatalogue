Marks of Childhood

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: January 26, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To help teens identify key characteristics of childhood faith, recognize Jesus' teaching on childlike qualities, and understand how to transition these into mature faith expressions.
1. Opening Icebreaker (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Childhood Memory Lane"

Teens share significant childhood memories using prompts:

Favorite toy/game
Best childhood treat
Favorite childhood story
Earliest happy memory

Discussion: "Just as we remember physical childhood experiences, we all have spiritual childhood markers. Today we'll explore what Jesus said about childlike faith."

2. Jesus & Childhood (15 min)
15 min
A. Childlike vs. Childish Faith

Jesus celebrated childlike qualities:

  • Trusting dependence (Matthew 18:3-4)
  • Unfiltered wonder and joy
  • Authentic expressions
"Truly I tell you, unless you change and become like little children, you will never enter the kingdom of heaven." Matthew 18:3
B. Marks of Spiritual Childhood

Characteristics to retain:

  • Immediate forgiveness
  • Wholehearted trust
  • Constant curiosity
  • Joyful worship
"From the lips of children and infants you have ordained praise." Psalm 8:2
C. Growing Without Losing

How to mature while keeping childlike faith:

  • Maintain wonder at God's creation
  • Keep simple trust in God's goodness
  • Express faith authentically
"When I was a child, I talked like a child... When I became a man, I put the ways of childhood behind me." 1 Corinthians 13:11
3. Faith Growth Activity (20 min)
20 min
Activity: "From Seed to Tree"

Teens create a visual representation of their faith growth:

Roots: Childhood Foundations

Identify early spiritual influences:

  • Who planted faith seeds?
  • What Bible stories shaped you?
  • Early prayer experiences
Trunk: Current Growth

Examine present faith development:

  • How is your faith strengthening?
  • What challenges are shaping you?
  • Current spiritual practices
Branches: Future Fruit

Envision mature faith expressions:

  • What spiritual fruit do you want to bear?
  • How will you serve others?
  • What legacy will you leave?

Facilitator Note: Provide tree templates and art supplies. Encourage sharing in small groups.

4. Reflection & Closing (10 min)
10 min
Childlike Faith Commitment

Guide teens to write a letter to their childhood self:

"What would you thank your younger self for believing?"
"What childhood faith qualities do you want to keep?"
"What advice would you give about growing in faith?"

Weekly Challenge

Practice one childlike faith quality this week: spontaneous prayer, awe at creation, or joyful worship.

"He called a little child to him, and placed the child among them. And he said: 'Truly I tell you, unless you change and become like little children, you will never enter the kingdom of heaven.'" Matthew 18:2-3

Closing Prayer: "Jesus, thank you for the gift of childhood faith. Help us retain its beautiful qualities even as we grow. Give us childlike trust in your goodness, wonder at your creation, and joy in your presence. Guide us as we grow into the mature believers you call us to be. Amen."

Bonus Tips for Facilitators
  • Bring childhood photos of yourself to share
  • Create a "Wall of Wonder" with photos of God's creation
  • Play "Wonder" by Hillsong United during the activity
  • Provide simple snacks from childhood (animal crackers, juice boxes)
  • Share a story of a child's profound faith insight
