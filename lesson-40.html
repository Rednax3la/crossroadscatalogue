Understanding Love

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: October 4, 2026
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To explore the biblical concept of love, understand different types of love, and teach teens how to express love in healthy, Christ-centered ways.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Love in Action"

Teens share examples of love they've witnessed or experienced:

"A time when someone showed me unexpected love..."
"A way I've shown love to someone recently..."
"What 'love' means to me personally..."

Discussion: "Love is more than a feeling - it's an action. Today we'll explore God's design for love."

2. Biblical Love (15 min)
15 min
A. God's Definition of Love

1 Corinthians 13:4-7 breakdown:

  • Patient and kind
  • Not envious, boastful, or proud
  • Honors others, not self-seeking
  • Not easily angered, keeps no record of wrongs
  • Protects, trusts, hopes, perseveres
"Love is patient, love is kind. It does not envy, it does not boast, it is not proud." 1 Corinthians 13:4
B. Four Greek Words for Love

Understanding different types of love:

Agape
Selfless, divine love
Phileo
Brotherly friendship
Storge
Family affection
Eros
Romantic love
"Above all, love each other deeply, because love covers over a multitude of sins." 1 Peter 4:8
C. Jesus as the Model of Love

How Jesus demonstrated love:

  • Sacrificial service (John 13:1-17)
  • Compassion for the marginalized
  • Forgiving those who hurt him
  • Ultimate sacrifice on the cross
"Greater love has no one than this: to lay down one's life for one's friends." John 15:13

Love in Action Challenge

Express love in someone else's language this week

4. Love Commitment (10 min)
10 min
Commitment to Love

My Love Commitment

I commit to expressing Christlike love by:

Family

Honoring parents, respecting siblings

Friends

Being loyal, trustworthy, and encouraging

Community

Serving others, showing compassion

Self

Practicing self-respect and healthy boundaries

"Dear children, let us not love with words or speech but with actions and in truth." 1 John 3:18

Closing Prayer: "Heavenly Father, thank you for your perfect love that never fails. Teach us to love as you love - with patience, kindness, and selflessness. Help us to see others through your eyes and to express love in ways that honor you. May our lives reflect your love to a world in need. Amen."
