Salvation & Easter Sleepover

Duration: Overnight
Date: April 13, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To explore the meaning of salvation through the Easter story in a memorable sleepover setting, helping teens understand Christ's sacrifice and resurrection while building community through shared experiences.
1. Welcome & Icebreakers (7:00 PM)
60 min
Sleepover Kickoff: "Salvation Scavenger Hunt"

Teams search for Easter-themed items that represent parts of the salvation story:

Bread (Last Supper)
Coins (Judas' betrayal)
Cross (Crucifixion)
Stone (Empty Tomb)

Discussion: "Each item represents part of the Easter story that brings us salvation. Tonight we'll explore how Christ's sacrifice gives us new life."

2. Salvation & Easter Teaching (8:00 PM)
60 min
A. The Problem: Sin

Understanding our need for salvation:

  • Separation from God (Romans 3:23)
  • Consequences of sin
  • Our inability to save ourselves
"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." Romans 3:23
B. The Solution: Christ's Sacrifice

How Easter provides salvation:

"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life." John 3:16
C. Our Response: Faith

The path to salvation:

Acknowledge Sin
Believe in Christ
Confess Faith
Be Baptized
3. Small Group Activities (9:00 PM)
90 min
Campfire Conversations
9:00 PM

Resurrection Reenactment: Small groups create short dramas of resurrection appearances

9:30 PM

S'mores Theology: Discuss salvation while making s'mores - how ingredients represent aspects of faith

10:00 PM

Salvation Testimonies: Leaders share personal salvation experiences

Prayer Stations
Cross Station

Write sins on paper, nail to cross

Empty Tomb Station

Leave prayers in "tomb" to be resurrected

Scripture Station

Meditate on salvation verses

4. Sunrise Devotion (6:00 AM)
60 min
Resurrection Celebration

New Life Commitment

As the sun rises, teens are invited to:

Accept Christ
Recommit to Faith
Be Baptized
Share Testimony
"He is not here; he has risen! Remember how he told you, while he was still with you in Galilee: 'The Son of Man must be delivered over to the hands of sinners, be crucified and on the third day be raised again.'" Luke 24:6-7
6:30 AM

Resurrection Breakfast: Share a meal while discussing what new life in Christ means

Sleepover Tips for Facilitators
  • Create a "Resurrection Garden" with stones, plants, and a tomb
  • Use glow sticks to represent "The Light of the World"
  • Provide Easter-themed snacks: empty tomb rolls, resurrection cookies
  • Have blankets and pillows for comfortable discussion areas
  • Prepare quiet spaces for reflection and prayer
  • Ensure proper safety and supervision throughout
