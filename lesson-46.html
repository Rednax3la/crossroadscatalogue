Movie Session

Duration: ~90 minutes
Date: December 6, 2026
Age Group: 13–14 years
Objective: To celebrate the year with a meaningful shared film experience that reinforces key spiritual themes, sparking discussion and reflection on the journey of faith.
1. Setting Up (15 min)
15 min
Create the Atmosphere

Transform the space into a cosy, relaxed setting:

Arrange seating casually — cushions and blankets welcome
Prepare popcorn and light snacks
Briefly recap 3–5 key highlights from Crossroads 2026

Opening prompt: Ask 2–3 teens: “What was your standout moment from Crossroads this year?”

2. Suggested Films (All Under 90 Min)
70–90 min
Joseph: King of Dreams (2000)
DreamWorks Animation • 74 minutes • Rated G
Animated story of Joseph — betrayed by his brothers, sold into slavery, yet never losing faith in God. A powerful story of perseverance, forgiveness, and divine purpose.
Dreams & AmbitionsForgivenessIdentityGod’s Plan
The Encounter (2010)
Pure Flix • 86 minutes • Rated PG
Five strangers stranded at a diner encounter a mysterious man who reveals himself to be Jesus. Each person faces deep questions about faith, purpose, and life choices.
SalvationDecision MakingLife as a Gift
The Pilgrim’s Progress (2019)
Animated • 85 minutes • Rated G
Faithful animated adaptation of John Bunyan’s classic allegory. Christian journeys from the City of Destruction to the Celestial City, overcoming temptations and trials along the way.
RedemptionResilienceFaith Journey
The Miracle Maker (2000)
Stop-motion Animation • 90 minutes • Rated G
A beautifully crafted stop-motion film about the life of Jesus, seen through the eyes of Tamar, a sick child He heals. Covers the full Gospel story with emotional depth.
SalvationEasterRedemption

Facilitator tip: Preview the chosen film beforehand and prepare 3–5 discussion questions tailored to your group.

3. Post-Film Discussion (20 min)
20 min
Reflecting Together
“Which character did you most relate to, and why?”
“What moment stood out to you most?”
“How does this film connect to our lessons this year?”
“What truth will you carry forward from this film?”
4. Year-End Celebration (15 min)
15 min
Closing the Year

Invite each teen to share:

One thing I learned about myself this year
One area where I grew in faith
My one word for 2027

Into 2027

Choose a word of the year — one word that captures the kind of person you want to become as you continue growing in faith.

“Forgetting what is behind and straining toward what is ahead, I press on toward the goal to win the prize for which God has called me heavenward in Christ Jesus.” Philippians 3:13–14

Closing Prayer: “Lord, thank you for this year — every lesson, every challenge, every moment of growth. Help us carry what we have learned close to our hearts. May we walk with you, serve others, and become more fully the people you created us to be. Amen.”
