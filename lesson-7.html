Creation: Planning & Time Management

Duration: 50 minutes
Date: February 16, 2025
Age Group: 13-14 years
Objective: To teach teens how God's creation reveals principles of planning and stewardship, and to develop practical time management skills based on biblical models of order and purpose.
1. Opening Activity (10 min)
10 min
Activity: "Creation Timeline"

Teens arrange creation events in order based on Genesis 1:

"Light and darkness separated"
"Sky and waters divided"
"Land and seas formed"
"Plants and trees created"
"Sun, moon, and stars placed"
"Birds and sea creatures made"
"Land animals and humans created"

Discussion: "Just as God created with order and purpose, we can approach our time with intentional planning. Notice how God worked in stages and rested."

2. Creation & Stewardship (15 min)
15 min
A. God as the Master Planner

Principles from Creation:

  • Orderly progression (Genesis 1:1-31)
  • Purposeful design (Psalm 19:1)
  • Strategic rest (Genesis 2:2-3)
  • Assigned responsibilities (Genesis 1:28)
"In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth. Now the earth was formless and empty, darkness was over the surface of the deep, and the Spirit of God was hovering over the waters." Genesis 1:1-2
B. Biblical Time Management

Key principles for stewardship of time:

  • Making the most of opportunities (Ephesians 5:15-16)
  • Seeking God's priorities first (Matthew 6:33)
  • Balancing work and rest (Exodus 20:8-11)
  • Planning with divine guidance (Proverbs 16:3)
"Teach us to number our days, that we may gain a heart of wisdom." Psalm 90:12
C. Time as Creation Stewardship

Managing time as part of God's created order:

  • Time as a gift from God
  • Using time to glorify God
  • Balancing responsibilities and rest
  • Planning as an act of worship

Planning Exercise: Teens create their own weekly schedule using priority badges, ensuring they include:

  • Spiritual growth time
  • Academic responsibilities
  • Family commitments
  • Rest and recreation
4. Commitment & Closing (10 min)
10 min
Stewardship Covenant

Weekly Time Stewardship

Commit to implementing your Creation-inspired planner this week:

  • Block time for God daily
  • Prioritize important over urgent
  • Include Sabbath rest
  • Review each evening
"Commit to the LORD whatever you do, and he will establish your plans." Proverbs 16:3

Closing Prayer: "Creator God, thank you for modeling perfect planning in your creation. Help us steward our time wisely as an act of worship. Give us discernment to prioritize what matters, discipline to follow through, and wisdom to include rest. May our schedules reflect your order and purpose. Amen."

Bonus Tips for Facilitators
  • Bring various planning tools (paper planners, digital apps)
  • Share your own weekly planning routine
  • Create a "Creation Timeline" mural on the wall
  • Use the 7 days of creation as a planning framework
  • Provide printed planner templates for teens to take home
